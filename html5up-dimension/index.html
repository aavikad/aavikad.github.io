About Me

I am a conservation biologist with a PhD in Zoology from the University of Oxford. My research focuses on the impacts of climate change and land-use change on bird communities in the eastern Himalayas.

I have worked as a Project Manager at the Ashoka Trust for Research in Ecology and the Environment (ATREE) and as a Doctoral Researcher at the University of Oxford. My work combines field research, data analysis, and community engagement to drive sustainable conservation efforts.

When I'm not in the field, I enjoy birdwatching, hiking, and painting.

Research Work

My research includes:

  • Climate Change Impacts: Evaluating the effects of climate change on bird communities in the eastern Himalayas using distribution models and field data.
  • Biodiversity Monitoring: Designing long-term biodiversity monitoring protocols for protected areas.
  • Community-Based Conservation: Collaborating with local communities and NGOs to implement conservation action plans.
  • Fieldwork: Conducted extensive fieldwork in Dibang Valley, eastern Indian Himalaya.
