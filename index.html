About

I am a conservation biologist with nearly a decade of fieldwork and research experience in the Eastern Himalaya and the Indo-Burman biodiversity hotspots in India. I use primary field data collection techniques such as point-counts, camera trapping, passive acoustic sampling, and microclimate monitoring to study the impacts of human-induced disturbances on the communities and distributions of birds, mammals, and insects. I am currently open to new opportunities in conservation and research fields.

In my free time, I enjoy birdwatching, going for hikes, practicing aerial arts, and reading.
